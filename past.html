HSC

The school I attended at had compacted-cirriculumn, meaning I would have to do my HSC throughout 2 years. This mean that the total subjects I had to do were split in half, where one year I would do one-half and the next year, the other half.

It had it's pros and cons but I think a major convinence is that you wouldn't need to remember as much content when doing the exams, since I was being examined for half my total subjects.

The following subjects I completed surprisingly don't include much tech-related content, and that's cause I had not planned to do computing science until later:

  • English Advanced
  • Maths Advanced
  • Biology
  • Chemistry
  • Physics
  • Maths Extension 1
  • Burger Preparing lvl 3

So yeah that is how it basically went with HSC. Being in my current position though, I probably would have chosen IT or something more relevant.


Oh and some cons of this compacted-cirriculumn would be that you get half as much time to study for your subjects (though you're only studying for half your subjects) so yeah.

You get thrown earlier at a time when you may not understand its seriousness, tho I think that con didn't affect me..just putting it out there to fill this page.
